Week One

The struggle, as they say, is real. If I had to characterize my experience in one word, it would be "stressful." I didn't know what to expect, and generally try to avoid expectations in favor of anticipation, but I was nonetheless thrown somewhat for a loop.
That's not necessarily a bad thing, though. It means I've learned the reality of what I'm in for, which means I can now set my expectations accordingly. And this page should be evidence enough that I managed to grasp some programming basics as well.
The introductory materials did not disappoint in what they predicted. I did want to quit, a couple different times. But now I'm glad I didn't. The material wasn't as hard as I'd convinced myself it was. And though my page will be far from complete, or even "nice" for that matter, for demo, it is at least a beginning, and I am happy enough with that for now.
They say the scariest thing for artists and writers is a blank page. I think the same thing is true for programming. Having now put pen to paper, if you will, the hardest part is done. I look forward to learning more, the better to build on this beginning.

Week Two

In general, the second week has, for me, gone more smoothly than the first. Somehow, I felt like I had more time to do what I needed to do. That's not to say it was without stress, but I suppose that's fairly normal when learning and applying new things.
The excercise in Pseudocoding proved to be an interesting one. It is a part of the practice of programming that I never knew existed until just recently, though in retrospect it makes a lot of sense that one would try to break up a problem into component parts. I was happy to discover that this logical excercise played to my strengths as a philosophy student, and thereby did not throw me off much at all. It was still a challenge, certainly, but one which I was equipped in advance to handle.
Though it did help that I got to pick my project. I always enjoy that.
As before, I remain interested to see how new materials will allow us to build on what's already been done.

Week Three

So this week started out maybe more stressful than the first. Not sure if it was something in the water or what, but from the end of last week, it seemed as though any confidence had gone straight out of me.
Thankfully, I was able to learn (all over again) that asking for help is my best weapon through this process. I think that maybe my biggest challenge is that I'm no longer used to pursuing things past the point of being bad at them, and what I've experienced are the growing pains of growing into that role of student again. Whatever things I've learned in the last few years have mostly been simple and easy to get used to. Hard things, as I'm relearning, are hard.
The hard work is slowly starting to pay off, too. While it's still true that I don't necessarily grasp everything on the first, or even second, attempt, I do grasp more now than I did at the beginning, and I think I'm starting to become used to the idea of experimenting around to find a workable solution to my coding problems. Still, must always remind myself to seek help when I'm driving myself mad. For this week, there are some who certainly have earned a shout-out for their invaluable assistance (they know who they are!).
So again I find myself in the position of saying that, having wanted to quit, I'm once more glad that I did not. And not to sound too egomaniacal, but I'm now looking forward to seeing what I can overcome next.

Week Four

This has been a much better week. After a rocky start on Monday, I rediscovered the benefits of actually being on-site, and the immediacy of assistance. It's probably not exaggerating by much at all to suggest that I may not have completed my projects this week if I had not left the house. Thankfully, it seems to be finally sinking in for me that help is not only available, but is desirable to seek out.
Now that my work and support habits/needs are more in order, it's a deal easier to have a little fun. I still find javascript to be a bit of a pain, but I suppose that's to be expected when learning a new language in tandem with a new way to structure one's thinking. It being a pain does not, however, suggest that I'm not starting to get it; while, starting out, it was easy enough to identify an individual expression, now it's getting easier to identify what the expression is saying. And not to beat a dead horse, but I would probably not be where I am without help (which I reiterate mostly for my own benefit).
I'm happy to say that I have very few misgivings about going forward into full-time. I eagerly anticipate a larger one-to-one environment, with lots of people to bounce ideas off of and generally collaborate with.
See you next week!

Week Five (Full-Time Week One)

The setting makes the difference. I know I've already said it, but the whole work-from-home deal was just not doing it for me. Actually being in the office, and now actually having a bunch of people around as well, is a game-changer. And as I get more comfortable with everyone, everything else seems to be flowing that much smoother.
That's not to say things haven't still been challenging, because they have. The weight of the challenge, however, is slowly becoming subsumed by the enjoyment of figuring something out and applying it. Case in point: this week's projects provided a nice mix of creative and logical challenges that overlapped well, and the local network of support made solving the various problems that arose a deal less arduous. Though it was always clear that I should reach out for help if I needed it, I didn't always feel right about seeking guidance.
Weirdly, being in-person seems to have taken the ego out of it for me. Id've thought the reverse.
I can't go without mentioning the session in the park with Dr. Luke, which was a very positive experience. Having the words to describe my goals in showing up every day makes the hard parts a little easier to put up with. Rather than laboring in the service of some vague potential that's "over there," I now have something more solid to latch onto.
I think that about does it for now. I'm looking forward to converting this blog over to bootstrap, and generally getting my hands dirty elsewhere.
Until next time.
