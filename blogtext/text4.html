Week Four


This has been a much better week. After a rocky start on Monday, I rediscovered the benefits of actually being on-site, and the immediacy of assistance. It's probably not exaggerating by much at all to suggest that I may not have completed my projects this week if I had not left the house. Thankfully, it seems to be finally sinking in for me that help is not only available, but is desirable to seek out.
Now that my work and support habits/needs are more in order, it's a deal easier to have a little fun. I still find javascript to be a bit of a pain, but I suppose that's to be expected when learning a new language in tandem with a new way to structure one's thinking. It being a pain does not, however, suggest that I'm not starting to get it; while, starting out, it was easy enough to identify an individual expression, now it's getting easier to identify what the expression is saying. And not to beat a dead horse, but I would probably not be where I am without help (which I reiterate mostly for my own benefit).
I'm happy to say that I have very few misgivings about going forward into full-time. I eagerly anticipate a larger one-to-one environment, with lots of people to bounce ideas off of and generally collaborate with.
See you next week!
