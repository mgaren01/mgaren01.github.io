Week Five (Full-Time Week One)


The setting makes the difference. I know I've already said it, but the whole work-from-home deal was just not doing it for me. Actually being in the office, and now actually having a bunch of people around as well, is a game-changer. And as I get more comfortable with everyone, everything else seems to be flowing that much smoother.
That's not to say things haven't still been challenging, because they have. The weight of the challenge, however, is slowly becoming subsumed by the enjoyment of figuring something out and applying it. Case in point: this week's projects provided a nice mix of creative and logical challenges that overlapped well, and the local network of support made solving the various problems that arose a deal less arduous. Though it was always clear that I should reach out for help if I needed it, I didn't always feel right about seeking guidance.
Weirdly, being in-person seems to have taken the ego out of it for me. Id've thought the reverse.
I can't go without mentioning the session in the park with Dr. Luke, which was a very positive experience. Having the words to describe my goals in showing up every day makes the hard parts a little easier to put up with. Rather than laboring in the service of some vague potential that's "over there," I now have something more solid to latch onto.
I think that about does it for now. I'm looking forward to converting this blog over to bootstrap, and generally getting my hands dirty elsewhere.
Until next time.
